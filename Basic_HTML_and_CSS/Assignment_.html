SAFETY TIPS FOR BEGINNING OUTDOOR CLIMBERS

BEST CLIMBING CONDITIONS

The best conditions to climb in are the conditions in which a climber can perform the best.
This is for two reasons:
1: Safety!
2: Fun!
If a climber wants to be as safe as possible, they will climb in optimal conditions.
Example of bad conditions:
A climber wakes up at 3:00 am
They are tired and decide it's a good idea to climb an easy route.
The climber starts climbing on an easy route.
Because they are climbing with a headlamp, they can't see very far.
The climber hears a rock falling from above but can't see it coming.
The climber doesn't know if they are going to be hit or if their rope will get cut.
The climbers belayer also doesn't know if the rock is going to hit them.
Both the climber and the belayer have chosen poor conditions to climb in.

CHOOSING A PARTNER

The best way to choose a good climbing partner is to find one with more experience than yourself.
You probably don't want to find someone who is going to put you in high-risk situations either.
Because of the discrepancy in levels of experience, communication to the experienced climber is vital.
The things an experienced climber will consider high-risk and the things you consider high-risk will vary.
As a beginner, you may not be aware of the risk that surrounds you.
This is something to take into consideration if you are not comfortable, but your experienced partner is not.
Choosing terrain that is uncomfortable to you, but not to your partner is also something to consider.
You may feel like it's unsafe, but your partner may have the experience to help you see why it is safe.
Either way, communications between partners is vital, so try and communicate early and often when choosing a partner.
